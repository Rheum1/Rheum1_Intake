What is Gout?

Gout is an inflammatory arthritis caused by excess uric acid forming crystals in the joints. It often strikes suddenly and causes intense pain and swelling, typically in one joint at a time.

Common Symptoms

  • Sudden, severe joint pain (often big toe, ankle, or knee)
  • Redness, warmth, and swelling
  • Tenderness that makes walking difficult
  • Attacks that last a few days to a week

How the Survey Helps
