Understanding Fibromyalgia

Fibromyalgia is a chronic condition characterized by widespread pain, fatigue, sleep disturbances, and cognitive difficulties often referred to as "fibro fog." It affects how your brain processes pain signals, amplifying painful sensations.

Key Features:

  • Widespread musculoskeletal pain
  • Fatigue and sleep disturbances
  • Cognitive difficulties ("fibro fog")
  • Often co-occurs with tension headaches, irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), anxiety, and depression
  • Tender points throughout the body

Common Treatment Approaches:

  • Medications (pain relievers, antidepressants, anti-seizure drugs)
  • Physical therapy and gentle exercise
  • Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)
  • Stress reduction techniques
  • Sleep hygiene practices
