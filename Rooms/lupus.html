Understanding Lupus

Systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) is a chronic autoimmune disease in which the immune system attacks its own tissues, causing widespread inflammation and tissue damage in affected organs.

Key Features:

  • Butterfly-shaped rash across the cheeks and nose
  • Joint pain, stiffness, and swelling
  • Fatigue and fever
  • Photosensitivity (sensitivity to sunlight)
  • Hair loss and mouth sores
  • Potential organ involvement (kidneys, heart, lungs)

Common Treatment Approaches:

  • Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs)
  • Antimalarial drugs (hydroxychloroquine)
  • Corticosteroids
  • Immunosuppressants
  • Biologics
  • Lifestyle modifications (sun protection, stress management)
